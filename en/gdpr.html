GDPR compliance

Last updated: May 2, 2026

1. Compliance approach

BORTEX is designed with privacy, data minimisation and operational control in mind. Compliance work covers how data is collected, stored, secured, accessed and deleted across the public website and connected product experiences.

2. Data protection principles

  • Purpose limitation for product, support and security operations.
  • Storage limitation and access control based on operational need.
  • Integrity and confidentiality through technical and organisational safeguards.
  • Transparency around lawful basis and rights management.

3. Security controls

Security measures may include encrypted transport, session controls, account protections, role-based permissions, logging and limited exposure of public endpoints. Specific controls can evolve with the product and deployment environment.

Where third-party processors are involved, they should be selected and configured with adequate contractual and technical safeguards.

4. Data-subject rights

Users may request access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction or objection where applicable under GDPR. Requests are assessed in light of contractual obligations, legal retention needs and system integrity requirements.

Requests can be sent to info@bortex.site.

This page is intended as a practical compliance summary. It may be updated as the product, infrastructure or legal requirements evolve.
